Idea & Goals

Health behaviors have been studied extensively and much is known. We eat more when tired, sad, depressed, when with friends, when out, in the evenings etc. We make up to 200 food and drink decisions per day, when to eat, what to eat, when to stop, another row of cookies, another drink, more water etc.

All these behaviors are functions of our mental reserves, our inherent cognitive ability to make rational decisions. Studied extensively in behavioral economics, rational decisions worsen when mental reserves deplete as a result of stress, lack of sleep, poor sleep hygiene.

We believe that we can use data from our ever-digitalized world to support healthier decisions, nudge people towards healthier behaviors, when they’re most susceptible to falling off the cliff.
