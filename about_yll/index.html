About Me

I'm a health services epidemiologist with about 10 years experience in studying patterns of injury and health care use among civilians and military populations. Currently I'm working on large cohort data, looking at care patterns and pathways, predictors of visits, misdiagnoses and such fun stuff. I also spend some time modeling the impact of state policies on injuries among teens , the elderly and military.

H2H Voting Comparator C-1000

A web based tool that compares, head-to-head (H2H), the voting records of US Senators or House Representatives.

New

H2H Voting Comparator C-1000, is a simple to use tool, that allows any two Senators or House Representatives to be compared. It provides a total tally of votes cast, number and percent of votes cast in agreement between the two and provides a list of bills where Senators or House Representatives agree or disagree. Also, the tool provides details on each Congressional member, on their membership in Committees or Subcommittees as well as providing a simple tracker of recently introduced bills as well as recently voted bills. The H2H comparison tool allows the public, journalists or other interested parties in better identifying shared interests between members of Congress, as well as identify areas/bills that they disagree. The tool allows to see whether members vote strictly on party line or whether areas of common interest exist.

Chronic Disease and Healthcare Cost Predictor

A few friends and I designed the Chronic Disease and Healthcare Cost Predictor tool as part of a HHS challenge. The tool is premised on the simple idea that if individuals are made immediately aware of their risk for disease and, importantly, the potential costs of disease, they may be compelled to make better choices with respect to their health. The tool uses data from the 2011 and 2012 Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System to model the probability of disease based on characteristics and risk factors input by the user. The probability values are then used as inputs into a cost model dervied from the 2012 Medical Expenditure Panel Survey to provide the user with their potential healthcare costs.
